## Near-Synonyms and Subtle Distinctions

**alleviate vs ameliorate vs mitigate:**
- alleviate: to make suffering less severe (temporary relief)
- ameliorate: to improve or make better (actual improvement)
- mitigate: to make less severe (reduce impact)

**apathetic vs impassive vs stoic:**
- apathetic: lacking interest or concern
- impassive: showing no emotion
- stoic: enduring pain without complaint

**careful vs meticulous vs scrupulous:**
- careful: taking care to avoid errors
- meticulous: extremely careful about details
- scrupulous: morally careful; conscientious

**calm vs placid vs serene:**
- calm: peaceful; not agitated
- placid: undisturbed; tranquil (often implies passivity)
- serene: calm and untroubled (positive connotation)

**criticize vs censure vs castigate:**
- criticize: to point out faults
- censure: to express severe disapproval
- castigate: to punish or criticize severely

**doubtful vs dubious vs skeptical:**
- doubtful: uncertain; unlikely
- dubious: questionable; causing doubt
- skeptical: habitually questioning; not easily convinced

**frugal vs parsimonious vs miserly:**
- frugal: economical (positive)
- parsimonious: excessively unwilling to spend (negative)
- miserly: hoarding money; extremely stingy (very negative)

**hamper vs hinder vs impede vs obstruct:**
- hamper: to restrict movement or progress
- hinder: to create difficulties or delay
- impede: to slow progress
- obstruct: to block or close off

**lessen vs diminish vs reduce:**
- lessen: to make less in size, amount, or degree
- diminish: to make smaller or less important
- reduce: to make smaller in size, amount, or extent

**praise vs laud vs extol:**
- praise: to express approval
- laud: to praise highly
- extol: to praise enthusiastically

**prevent vs preclude vs forestall:**
- prevent: to keep from happening
- preclude: to make impossible
- forestall: to prevent by taking action in advance

**reckless vs rash vs impetuous:**
- reckless: careless of consequences
- rash: acting without due consideration
- impetuous: acting on impulse

**withhold vs retain vs reserve:**
- withhold: to refuse to give
- retain: to keep possession of
- reserve: to set aside for future use

## Connotation vs Denotation

**Positive vs Neutral vs Negative Connotations:**

**Thin:**
- Positive: slender, svelte, trim, lean
- Neutral: thin, slim
- Negative: scrawny, gaunt, emaciated, skeletal

**Fat:**
- Positive: plump, full-figured
- Neutral: heavy, large
- Negative: obese, corpulent, rotund

**Proud:**
- Positive: confident, self-assured, dignified
- Neutral: proud
- Negative: arrogant, haughty, conceited, vain

**Careful with money:**
- Positive: frugal, thrifty, economical, prudent
- Neutral: careful, cautious
- Negative: stingy, miserly, parsimonious, cheap

**Talkative:**
- Positive: articulate, eloquent, expressive
- Neutral: talkative, communicative
- Negative: garrulous, loquacious, verbose, long-winded

**Childlike:**
- Positive: youthful, innocent, childlike
- Neutral: young, juvenile
- Negative: childish, immature, puerile

**Determined:**
- Positive: resolute, steadfast, tenacious, persistent
- Neutral: determined
- Negative: stubborn, obstinate, pigheaded, intransigent

## Words Commonly Confused on the GRE

**adverse vs averse:**
- adverse: harmful; unfavorable (adverse conditions)
- averse: opposed to; unwilling (averse to change)

**affect vs effect:**
- affect: to influence (v) / emotion (n)
- effect: result (n) / to bring about (v)

**allude vs elude:**
- allude: to refer to indirectly
- elude: to escape; to avoid

**appraise vs apprise:**
- appraise: to evaluate
- apprise: to inform

**complement vs compliment:**
- complement: to complete; to enhance
- compliment: to praise

**continual vs continuous:**
- continual: repeated regularly
- continuous: without interruption

**disinterested vs uninterested:**
- disinterested: impartial; unbiased
- uninterested: not interested; indifferent

**elicit vs illicit:**
- elicit: to draw out
- illicit: illegal

**eminent vs imminent vs immanent:**
- eminent: distinguished; prominent
- imminent: about to happen
- immanent: inherent; existing within

**flaunt vs flout:**
- flaunt: to display ostentatiously
- flout: to disregard; to violate

**ingenious vs ingenuous:**
- ingenious: clever; inventive
- ingenuous: innocent; naive

**prescribe vs proscribe:**
- prescribe: to recommend; to order
- proscribe: to forbid; to condemn

**principle vs principal:**
- principle: fundamental truth or law
- principal: main; most important / head of school

**discreet vs discrete:**
- discreet: careful; prudent
- discrete: separate; distinct

**respectfully vs respectively:**
- respectfully: with respect
- respectively: in the order given
