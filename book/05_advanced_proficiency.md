# 5. Advanced Proficiency (C1)

**Chapter summary:** Near-native fluency with sophisticated grammar, idiomatic expressions, and the ability to express subtle meanings.

## Future Perfect and Future Continuous

**Future perfect:** Subject + will have + past participle
- By next year, I will have graduated
- She will have finished by 5pm
- Use: action completed before a future time

**Future continuous:** Subject + will be + verb-ing
- This time tomorrow, I'll be flying to Paris
- We'll be waiting for you at the station
- Use: action in progress at a future time

**Future perfect continuous:** will have been + verb-ing
- By December, I'll have been working here for 10 years
- Use: emphasize duration up to a future point

## Advanced Conditionals

**Zero conditional variations:** general truths with modals
- If you add salt to water, it can preserve food

**Inverted conditionals:** (formal)
- Should you need help, call me (= If you should need...)
- Had I known, I would have acted (= If I had known...)
- Were I rich, I'd buy a yacht (= If I were rich...)

**Alternatives to 'if':**
- Unless (if not): Unless you hurry, you'll be late
- Provided/Providing that: I'll go provided that you come too
- As long as: You can stay as long as you're quiet
- Suppose/Supposing: Suppose he doesn't come, what then?
- In case: Take an umbrella in case it rains (preparation)

## Subjunctive Mood

**Present subjunctive:** base form after certain verbs/expressions
- I suggest that he go (not goes)
- It's essential that she be present (not is)
- The doctor recommended that he stop smoking

**Common triggers:** suggest, recommend, insist, demand, require, request, propose, important, essential, vital, necessary, imperative

**Past subjunctive:** were for all persons
- If I were you, I'd accept the offer
- I wish I were taller
- He acts as if he were the boss

## Cleft Sentences

**It-clefts:** emphasize specific information
- It was Maria who called (not John)
- It's money that I need (not advice)
- It wasn't until midnight that he arrived

**What-clefts:** emphasize action/state
- What I want is some peace and quiet
- What bothers me is his attitude
- What happened was unexpected

**Wh-clefts variations:**
- All I need is time
- The thing (that) I like most is...
- The reason why I left was...

## Advanced Participle Clauses

**Present participle:** simultaneous/reason
- Walking down the street, I saw my old friend
- Being tired, she went to bed early
- Not knowing what to do, he called for help

**Past participle:** passive meaning
- Written in 1605, the play remains popular
- Exhausted by the journey, we slept immediately

**Perfect participle:** completed action before main verb
- Having finished my work, I went home
- Having been warned, they were careful

**With their own subject:**
- The test being too difficult, most students failed
- Weather permitting, we'll have a picnic

## Nominalization

**Convert verbs to nouns:** creates formal/academic style
- discover → discovery (The discovery of DNA was revolutionary)
- move → movement (The movement of goods requires planning)
- fail → failure (His failure to attend was noted)

**Convert adjectives to nouns:**
- important → importance (The importance of education cannot be overstated)
- different → difference (The difference between them is minimal)

**Common suffixes:** -tion, -ment, -ness, -ity, -ance, -ence, -al, -ure
**Effect:** more concise, formal, abstract

## Discourse Markers

**Structuring:**
- Firstly, Secondly, Finally
- To begin with, Furthermore, In addition
- On the one hand, On the other hand
- With regard to, Concerning, As for

**Contrast:**
- Nevertheless, Nonetheless, Even so
- Having said that, That said
- Conversely, By contrast

**Result:**
- Consequently, Hence, Thus, Accordingly
- As a result, As a consequence

**Clarification:**
- In other words, That is to say, Namely
- To put it another way

## Collocation and Fixed Expressions

**Strong collocations:** words that go together
- make: a decision, an effort, progress, a mistake, sense
- do: homework, business, damage, your best, research
- have: an effect, an impact, a chat, doubts, access
- take: place, action, advantage, responsibility, turns

**Fixed expressions:**
- at the end of the day (ultimately)
- by and large (generally)
- for the time being (temporarily)
- in the long run (eventually)
- to cut a long story short (summarize)

**Intensifiers:**
- deeply concerned, highly unlikely, utterly impossible
- bitterly disappointed, totally agree, absolutely certain

## Register and Formality

**Formal:**
- Vocabulary: purchase, commence, terminate, assist
- Structures: passives, nominalization, complex sentences
- Avoid: contractions, phrasal verbs, personal pronouns
- Context: academic writing, business, official documents

**Informal:**
- Vocabulary: buy, start, end, help
- Structures: active voice, simple sentences, contractions
- Include: phrasal verbs, colloquialisms, personal tone
- Context: emails to friends, casual speech

**Neutral:** general communication, professional but friendly

**Shifting register:**
- I regret to inform you (formal) vs I'm sorry to say (informal)
- It is recommended that (formal) vs You should (informal)
