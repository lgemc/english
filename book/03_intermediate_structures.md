# 3. Intermediate Structures (B1)

**Chapter summary:** Developing more complex grammar and expanding vocabulary to handle most everyday situations and express opinions.

## Present Perfect Tense

**Form:** Subject + have/has + past participle
- I have worked / I've worked
- She has seen / She's seen

**Uses:**
- Past actions with present relevance (I've lost my keys)
- Life experiences (Have you ever been to Paris?)
- Actions starting in past, continuing now (I've lived here for 5 years)

**Time expressions:** ever, never, already, yet, just, for, since, so far
**Negative:** haven't/hasn't + past participle
**Questions:** Have/Has + subject + past participle?

## Past Continuous Tense

**Form:** Subject + was/were + verb-ing
- I was sleeping
- They were working

**Uses:**
- Actions in progress at a specific past time (At 8pm, I was watching TV)
- Background actions (It was raining when I left)
- Interrupted actions (I was cooking when she called)

**Pattern:** While + past continuous, past simple

## Future Tenses (will, going to)

**Will:** predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises, offers
- It will rain tomorrow
- I'll help you
- Form: will + base verb

**Going to:** plans, intentions, predictions with evidence
- I'm going to study medicine
- Look at those clouds - it's going to rain
- Form: am/is/are + going to + base verb

**Present continuous for future:** arranged plans (I'm meeting Tom tomorrow)

## Conditionals (Zero, First, Second)

**Zero conditional:** general truths
- If + present, present
- If you heat water to 100°C, it boils

**First conditional:** real future possibility
- If + present, will + verb
- If it rains, I will stay home

**Second conditional:** unreal/unlikely present/future
- If + past simple, would + verb
- If I won the lottery, I would travel the world

## Passive Voice (Introduction)

**Form:** Subject + be + past participle (+ by agent)
- Active: Shakespeare wrote Hamlet
- Passive: Hamlet was written by Shakespeare

**Uses:** focus on action/object, unknown/unimportant agent
**Common tenses:**
- Present simple: is/are + past participle (English is spoken here)
- Past simple: was/were + past participle (The car was stolen)

## Relative Clauses

**Defining:** essential information, no commas
- The book that I bought is interesting
- The man who called is my boss

**Non-defining:** extra information, use commas
- My car, which is red, is very fast
- Tom, who lives next door, is a doctor

**Pronouns:** who (people), which (things), that (both), where (places), when (time)
**Object pronouns:** can be omitted (The book [that] I read)

## Reported Speech (Basics)

**Tense changes:**
- Present simple → Past simple (He said he liked it)
- Present continuous → Past continuous (She said she was working)
- Past simple → Past perfect (He said he had seen it)
- Will → Would (They said they would come)

**Other changes:**
- now → then
- today → that day
- tomorrow → the next day
- this → that

**Pattern:** He said (that) + clause

## Connectors and Linking Words

**Addition:** and, also, in addition, furthermore, moreover
**Contrast:** but, however, although, even though, whereas, while
**Cause/Effect:** because, so, therefore, as a result, consequently
**Time:** when, while, after, before, until, as soon as
**Examples:** for example, such as, for instance
**Conclusion:** in conclusion, to sum up, finally

**Usage:** improve coherence and flow in writing/speaking
