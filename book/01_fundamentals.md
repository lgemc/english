# 1. Fundamentals (A1-A2)

**Chapter summary:** Basic building blocks of English including the alphabet, pronunciation, basic grammar structures, and essential vocabulary for everyday situations.

## The English Alphabet and Pronunciation

**Alphabet:** A-Z (26 letters)
- Vowels: A, E, I, O, U
- Consonants: B, C, D, F, G, H, J, K, L, M, N, P, Q, R, S, T, V, W, X, Y, Z

**Key pronunciation rules:**
- Silent letters: know, write, honest, knight
- Common sounds: /θ/ (think), /ð/ (this), /ʃ/ (shop), /tʃ/ (church)

## Personal Pronouns and To Be

**Pronouns:** I, you, he, she, it, we, they

**To be:**
- I am / I'm
- You are / You're
- He/She/It is / He's/She's/It's
- We/They are / We're/They're

**Negative:** am not, isn't, aren't
**Questions:** Am I? Is he? Are they?

## Present Simple Tense

**Form:** Subject + verb (add -s/-es for he/she/it)
- I work, he works
- I go, she goes
- I study, it studies

**Uses:** habits, facts, routines
**Negative:** don't/doesn't + verb
**Questions:** Do/Does + subject + verb?

## Articles (a, an, the)

**A/An (indefinite):** a book, an apple
- Use "an" before vowel sounds

**The (definite):** the sun, the book I mentioned
- Specific or previously mentioned items

**No article:** general plural/uncountable nouns (I like music, Dogs are loyal)

## Basic Question Formation

**Yes/No questions:** Do you like coffee? Is he a teacher?
**Wh- questions:** What, Where, When, Who, Why, How
- What is your name?
- Where do you live?
- When does the class start?

## Common Nouns and Adjectives

**Nouns:** person, place, thing, idea
- Common: book, city, dog
- Proper: London, Maria (capitalize)

**Adjectives:** describe nouns
- Order: opinion-size-age-color-origin-material
- Example: a beautiful small old red Italian leather bag

## Numbers, Dates, and Time

**Numbers:**
- Cardinal: one, two, three...
- Ordinal: first, second, third...

**Dates:** January 1st, 2024 / 1st January 2024

**Time:**
- 3:00 = three o'clock
- 3:15 = quarter past three
- 3:30 = half past three
- 3:45 = quarter to four

## Essential Everyday Vocabulary

**Greetings:** Hello, Hi, Good morning/afternoon/evening, Goodbye, Bye
**Courtesy:** Please, Thank you, Sorry, Excuse me
**Family:** mother, father, sister, brother, son, daughter
**Colors:** red, blue, green, yellow, black, white
**Days:** Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, Sunday
**Months:** January, February, March, April, May, June, July, August, September, October, November, December
**Common verbs:** be, have, go, come, do, make, get, see, know, think, take, give, want, like, need
**Common adjectives:** good, bad, big, small, hot, cold, new, old, happy, sad
