# 2. Elementary Communication (A2)

**Chapter summary:** Expanding communication skills with more verb tenses, prepositions, and vocabulary for familiar topics and routine tasks.

## Present Continuous Tense

**Form:** Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
- I am working / I'm working
- She is reading / She's reading
- They are playing / They're playing

**Uses:** actions happening now, temporary situations, future arrangements
**Negative:** am not/isn't/aren't + verb-ing
**Questions:** Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?

## Past Simple Tense

**Regular verbs:** base + -ed (walked, played, studied)
**Irregular verbs:** saw, went, had, made, took, came, ate, wrote

**Uses:** completed actions in the past
**Negative:** didn't + verb
**Questions:** Did + subject + verb?

**Time expressions:** yesterday, last week/month/year, ago, in 2020

## Prepositions of Time and Place

**Time:**
- at: specific times (at 5pm, at night, at Christmas)
- on: days/dates (on Monday, on January 1st)
- in: months/years/periods (in May, in 2024, in the morning)

**Place:**
- at: specific points (at home, at the station)
- on: surfaces (on the table, on the wall)
- in: enclosed spaces (in the room, in London)
- Common: next to, between, behind, in front of, under, above

## Countable and Uncountable Nouns

**Countable:** can be counted (one book, two books)
- Use: a/an, numbers, many, few
- Example: a car, three apples, many students

**Uncountable:** cannot be counted (water, information, advice)
- Use: much, little, some, a piece of
- Example: some water, much time, a piece of advice

**Both:** some, any, a lot of

## Basic Comparatives and Superlatives

**Short adjectives:**
- Comparative: -er (bigger, taller, faster)
- Superlative: -est (biggest, tallest, fastest)

**Long adjectives:**
- Comparative: more + adjective (more beautiful, more expensive)
- Superlative: most + adjective (most beautiful, most expensive)

**Irregular:** good→better→best, bad→worse→worst, far→farther/further→farthest/furthest

**Patterns:** as...as (as tall as), not as...as (not as big as)

## Modal Verbs (can, could, should)

**Can:** ability, permission, requests
- I can swim
- Can I help you?

**Could:** past ability, polite requests, possibility
- I could run fast when I was young
- Could you help me?

**Should:** advice, recommendations
- You should study more
- Should I call him?

**Form:** modal + base verb (no -s, no to)
**Negative:** cannot/can't, could not/couldn't, should not/shouldn't

## Common Phrasal Verbs

**Separable:**
- turn on/off (the lights)
- pick up (the phone)
- put on (clothes)
- take off (shoes)

**Inseparable:**
- look for (search)
- look after (take care of)
- get up (wake up)
- go out (leave home)
- come back (return)
- run into (meet by chance)

**Usage:** verb + particle changes meaning (get = obtain, get up = wake)
