# 4. Upper Intermediate Mastery (B2)

**Chapter summary:** Refining language skills with advanced grammar, nuanced expressions, and the ability to discuss abstract topics.

## Present Perfect Continuous

**Form:** Subject + have/has + been + verb-ing
- I have been working / I've been working
- She has been studying / She's been studying

**Uses:**
- Actions that started in the past and continue now (I've been learning English for 5 years)
- Recent activities with present evidence (You've been crying - your eyes are red)
- Emphasis on duration of activity

**Time expressions:** for, since, all day/week, recently, lately
**Difference from present perfect:** continuous emphasizes duration/activity; simple emphasizes completion/result

## Past Perfect Tenses

**Past perfect simple:** Subject + had + past participle
- I had finished the work before she arrived
- They had already left when I called

**Uses:** action completed before another past action
**Time expressions:** before, after, already, just, never, by the time

**Past perfect continuous:** Subject + had + been + verb-ing
- I had been waiting for an hour when the bus finally came
- She was tired because she had been working all night

**Uses:** emphasize duration before a past moment

## Third Conditional and Mixed Conditionals

**Third conditional:** unreal past (regrets, criticism)
- If + past perfect, would have + past participle
- If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam

**Mixed conditionals:**
- Type 1: If + past perfect, would + base verb (past cause, present result)
  - If I had studied medicine, I would be a doctor now
- Type 2: If + past simple, would have + past participle (present/continuing cause, past result)
  - If I weren't afraid of heights, I would have climbed that mountain

## Advanced Modal Verbs (might, ought to, must)

**Might:** possibility, permission (less certain than may)
- It might rain later (50% possibility)
- You might want to check that

**Ought to:** advice, expectation, moral obligation
- You ought to see a doctor
- He ought to be here by now

**Must:**
- Strong obligation: You must wear a seatbelt
- Strong deduction: She must be tired (she worked all night)
- Negative: mustn't (prohibition) vs don't have to (no obligation)

**Past modals:**
- could have, should have, would have, must have, might have + past participle

## Passive Voice (All Forms)

**All tenses:**
- Present simple: is/are done
- Present continuous: is/are being done
- Past simple: was/were done
- Past continuous: was/were being done
- Present perfect: has/have been done
- Past perfect: had been done
- Future: will be done
- Modal: can/should/must be done

**Agent with by:** The house was built by my grandfather
**Common: omit agent when unknown/obvious:** The window was broken

## Advanced Phrasal Verbs

**Three-word:** get on with, look forward to, run out of, put up with, catch up with, come up with, look down on

**Inseparable:**
- go through (experience/examine)
- break down (stop working/lose control)
- carry out (perform/execute)
- come across (find by chance)
- set up (establish)

**Separable:**
- bring up (mention/raise children)
- make up (invent/reconcile)
- work out (solve/exercise)
- turn down (reject/reduce volume)

## Reported Speech (Advanced)

**Reporting questions:**
- Yes/no: He asked if/whether I was ready
- Wh-: She asked where I lived

**Reporting requests/commands:**
- Direct: "Please help me"
- Reported: He asked me to help him
- Negative: She told me not to go

**Reporting verbs:** say, tell, ask, suggest, admit, apologize, complain, explain, promise, refuse, warn, advise, recommend

**Pattern variation:** He suggested going / He suggested that we go

## Indirect Questions

**Form:** more polite than direct questions
- Direct: Where is the station?
- Indirect: Could you tell me where the station is?

**Word order:** question word + subject + verb (no inversion)
**Yes/no questions:** if/whether
- Do you know if/whether the shop is open?

**Common phrases:** Could you tell me...?, Do you know...?, I wonder..., I'd like to know...

## Emphasis and Inversion

**Cleft sentences with 'it':**
- It was John who called (not Mary)
- It's the blue one that I want

**Cleft sentences with 'what':**
- What I need is a holiday
- What she did was unacceptable

**Inversion after negatives:** (formal)
- Never have I seen such beauty
- Rarely does he arrive on time
- Not only did she win, but she also broke the record
- Under no circumstances should you open this door

**Emphasis with 'do/does/did':**
- I do like chocolate (contrary to expectation)
- She did finish the project (despite doubts)
